Magnetic resonance imaging is a medical imaging technique used in radiology to form pictures of the anatomy and the physiological processes of the body in both health and disease. MRI scanners use strong magnetic fields, Magnetic resonance imaging electric field gradients, and radio waves to generate images of the organs in the body. MRI does not involve X-rays and the use of ionising radiation, Magnetic resonance imaging which distinguishes it from CT or CAT scans. the organs in the body.
